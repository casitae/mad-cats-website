Food as a Conversation Between Cultures

RESERVE FOOD MENU

We believe food is about bringing people together. Drawing inspiration from the communal food cultures of the Mediterranean, Middle East and South-East Asia, our menu is designed for passing plates — trying a little of everything and discovering flavours together.

We wanted to create a space that feels familiar: a great record, a few shared dishes and another round. Whether you're here for a quick bite or a long evening, the experience is built around the idea that the best moments happen when they're shared.

We don't set out to recreate traditional dishes or follow a single cuisine. Instead we let the flavours, techniques and traditions that have shaped us meet in unexpected ways. As Head Chef Rafail Papimitoglou puts it, “It's a conversation between cultures.”

Lebanese spices might find their way into Chinese-style dumplings. Japanese techniques refine Middle Eastern ingredients. Greek influences sit alongside flavours from Singapore and beyond — familiar enough to feel comforting, yet layered enough to spark curiosity.

We cannot get enough of the hand-folded Shish Barak Dumplings — Lebanese seven-spiced meat wrapped in delicate Chinese-style dumpling dough. And the Devon Crab Brioche — fresh crab from East London's Fin & Flounder on crispy brioche, with smoked Napa cabbage, turnip and home-made Gochujang aioli.

Have an idea for the table? If you're a chef, cook, supper club, producer or food creative with an idea to share — from guest takeovers to collaborative dinners — we'd love to explore it. Drop us a line at rafail@madcats.uk.
